About Me

I am in my final year as a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at Washington University in St. Louis. As a graduate research assistant at the Visual Interface and Behavior Exploration Lab (VIBE), I investigate the interplay of information visualization and visual analytics under the supervision of Alvitta Ottley.

Research Interests

My research focuses on several interconnected areas that aim to make data visualization more effective and trustworthy:

Visualization Literacy

Understanding how people read and interpret visualizations across different cultural contexts

Trust in Visualization

Investigating factors that influence user trust in data visualizations

Misinformation in Visualizations

Detecting and preventing misleading visual representations of data

Computer Vision & AI for Visualization

Applying state-of-the-art vision models and multi-agentic LLMs to enhance visualization communication and understanding

Recent Projects

  • Benchmarking visual language models on standardized visualization literacy assessments
  • Developing multi-dimensional frameworks for measuring trust in data visualizations
  • Creating AI-powered tools that help users perceive and interpret visualizations more effectively
  • Investigating cross-cultural differences in visualization comprehension and design preferences

My research explores diverse applications of machine learning, artificial intelligence (including large language models), and computer vision in the context of data visualization. I'm particularly interested in developing AI-powered tools that help users perceive, understand, and effectively interpret visualizations across different cultural contexts. Central to my work is measuring visualization literacy and trust in visualization, ensuring that data representations are not only technically sound but also accessible and meaningful to diverse global populations.
